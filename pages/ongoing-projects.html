What I Look For

I value curiosity, independence, and a willingness to get your hands dirty with real systems. Strong programming skills (Python, C++) and experience with robotics frameworks (ROS2) are essential. Familiarity with SLAM/VIO, PyTorch, or computer vision is a plus. But beyond technical skills, I'm looking for people who think critically, communicate clearly, and are excited about solving real problems.

My Advising Philosophy

I believe in hands-on mentorship and giving students ownership of their projects. I provide guidance, resources, and regular feedback, but I also encourage independence and creative problem-solving. My goal is not just to produce papers, but to help you develop as a researcher and thinker. Past students I've worked with have been admitted to top PhD programs and research labs worldwide.

Available Positions

Research Assistants (Undergraduate/Master's)

Work on specific components of ongoing projects. Ideal for students looking to gain research experience and potentially co-author publications. Time commitment: 10-20 hours/week.

Final Year Project Students

Undertake a substantial research project as part of your degree. I have several project ideas in robot navigation, perception, and learning that can be tailored to your interests.

Visiting Researchers

Short-term (3-6 months) research collaborations for graduate students or researchers from other institutions. Must have prior research experience and clear project goals.

If you're interested in collaborating, reach out via email or connect with me on LinkedIn. Please include a brief description of your background and what you're interested in working on.
