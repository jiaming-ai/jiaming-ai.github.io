Project Highlights

Safe Navigation in Dynamic Environments

ICRA 2024

Developed a topological mapping approach that enables robots to navigate safely in crowded, dynamic environments. The system uses probabilistic inference to handle uncertainty and predict human motion patterns. This work received the Best Paper Award at ICRA 2024.

Key Contributions:

  • Novel topological representation that scales to large environments
  • Real-time human motion prediction with uncertainty quantification
  • Deployed and tested on physical robots in real-world scenarios

Vision-Language Models for Robot Tasks

CoRL 2023

Created a multimodal framework that enables robots to understand and execute tasks described in natural language. The system combines vision transformers with large language models to ground language instructions in visual perception.

Key Contributions:

  • End-to-end vision-language-action model for robot control
  • Zero-shot generalization to novel objects and tasks
  • Extensive evaluation on real robot manipulation tasks

Uncertainty-Aware SLAM

IROS 2023

Developed a SLAM system that explicitly models and propagates uncertainty throughout the mapping and localization pipeline. The approach improves robustness in challenging environments with sensor noise and dynamic obstacles.

Key Contributions:

  • Probabilistic framework for uncertainty quantification in SLAM
  • Robust loop closure detection under perceptual aliasing
  • Real-time performance on embedded systems
